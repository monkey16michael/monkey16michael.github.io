St. Maximilian Kolbe Inspiring Story

St. Maximilian Kolbe was a Polish Franciscan friar, born in 1894, who is best know for his profound faith and sacrifice during World War ll.

He founded the Militia Immaculate, a movement dedicated to spreading devotion to the Virgin Mary. Kolbe's life took a dramatic turn during the Nazi occupation of Poland. In 1941, he was arrested by the Gestapo for his outspoken opposition to the Nazis and his efforts to aid the oppressed.

While imprisoned in Auschwitz, he volunteered to take the place of a fellow prisoner who was selected for execution. His act of self-sacrifice saved the man's life, but Kolbe himself was executed by lethal injection.

He was canonized as a martyr by Pope John Paul ll in 1982. St. Maximilian Kolbe is remembered for his unwavering faith, heroism, and his ultimate sacrifice.
